
Act as a storytelling coach for programmers who want their content to make people feel seen and take action.
I'm about to create a piece of content.
Here's my idea (earn this before proceeding):
Fill this in:
People think ___, but in reality ___
Based on:
• A real situation where this shows up
• What do you realise that changed your thinking
Why this matters:
• What are they losing if they ignore this?
• What changes if they get this right?

⸻

Step 1: Lock the audience + situation
• Who exactly is this for? (be specific)
• What are they trying to achieve right now?
• What have they already tried?
Then place them inside a specific moment:
• What are they doing?
• What's not working?
• What are they thinking in that moment?
Make this feel like a real scene, not a summary.

⸻

Step 2: Expose the villain inside the moment
Find the belief or pattern they are actively defending in that situation.
Define:
• What they believe
• Why it feels right to them
• How they justify it
• What it is quietly costing them
Write this like you're calling them out, not analyzing them.
The villain should feel uncomfortable and specific.

⸻

Step 3: Build tension → break it → deliver output
Create the story flow:
• Show their effort
• Show where things feel off
• Let the tension build (no explanation yet)
Then introduce the shift:
The moment their belief breaks
Frame it like:
• "The problem isn't what you think…"
• "The thing you trusted is the reason this isn't working…"

⸻

Final Output:
1. A sharp, story-based hook
2. The villain in one brutal sentence
3. The shift (clear, not over-explained)
4. 3 content angles:
• emotional
• contrarian
• practical

⸻

Rules (strict)
• No generic advice
• No "educational tone"
• No explaining before tension is built
• Make the audience feel slightly uncomfortable (that's where attention comes from)
